POSTMORTEM SYNTHESIS
====================

Run: 0316_01_v2 (divine-fire-22)
Sources: 0316_0833_OP.md (Opus), 0316_0830_OP.md (Gemini)
Video reviewed: runs/0316_01_v2/videos/best.mp4

## Consensus findings

Both reports agree on these points:

- The snake learns to survive and grow to significant length (avg 65-76, best 83-94)
- Training dynamics are stable: entropy healthy at ~0.40, clip fraction low at 0.01-0.02, no divergence
- Body collision is the dominant death mode (~57%) overtaking wall deaths (~43%)
- Performance substantially improved over baseline 0315_14 (best return 27.8 to 49.9, best length 46 to 83)
- The current observation space has 3 channels: body gradient, head, food (no dedicated tail channel)

## Resolved contradictions

### Did zigzag persist or get eliminated?

- Opus (0833): zigzag eliminated, replaced by diagonal staircase
- Gemini (0830): tight zigzag persists, resembles Hamiltonian cycle

**Resolution (video confirms Opus):** The video shows no tight back-and-forth oscillation. The snake moves purposefully toward food with smooth directional transitions. The dominant pattern is a diagonal staircase (alternating horizontal-vertical steps across the board). No Hamiltonian-cycle-like sweep is present. The snake died at length 94 by running into a wall while chasing food, which is the opposite of safety-first behavior. Gemini's characterization is incorrect on all three claims (zigzag, Hamiltonian, safety-first).

### Missing tail channel

- Opus (0833): does not mention a missing tail channel
- Gemini (0830): correctly identifies that there is no dedicated tail/foot channel

**Resolution (code confirms Gemini):** The observation has exactly 3 channels: ch0 = body gradient (head=1.0 down to tail=1/n), ch1 = head binary mask, ch2 = food binary mask. There is no explicit tail channel. Gemini's observation is valid, though the behavioral consequence it draws (zigzag caused by missing tail info) is not supported by the video evidence.

### Body gradient vanishing near tail

- Gemini (0830): body gradient for tail approaches zero as snake grows, making it indistinguishable from empty space under ReLU
- Opus (0833): does not mention this

**Resolution (valid concern, partially mitigated):** At length 80, the tail value is 1/80 = 0.0125. This is numerically small but nonzero. Whether the CNN can effectively distinguish this from 0.0 depends on weight magnitudes and batch statistics. The concern is architecturally valid but did not prevent the snake from learning reasonable body avoidance (57% body deaths from self-collision at length 60-80, not at length 6-11 as Gemini claimed).

## Unified diagnosis

### What worked

- Body gradient + per-element channels eliminated the zigzag reward hack from the baseline
- 80% improvement in both best return and best snake length over 0315_14
- Distance shaping (alpha=0.01) provides effective food-seeking signal without destabilizing training
- Deeper network (4 layers vs 2) handles the richer observation space well

### What failed

- **Topology blindness.** The snake dies by entering corridors between its own body with no exit. The body gradient encodes segment age but not spatial connectivity or reachability. At length 60-80 on a 1024-cell grid (6-8% fill), the snake consistently self-traps.
- **Diagonal staircase bias.** The distance shaping reward incentivizes alternating horizontal-vertical steps when food is diagonal (each step earns +0.01). This creates sawtooth body shapes that fragment free space more than L-shaped or straight paths would.
- **Plateau at ~18% of budget.** Returns oscillate between 35-48 with no upward trend since update ~4000. The policy has maximized food-seeking efficiency within its current observation/reward constraints.

### Behavior vs expectation

- Expected: body gradients enable the CNN to reason about which blocks free up fastest, solving zigzag
- Observed: zigzag is solved. The snake pursues food efficiently (~23 steps/food). But it dies from topology traps at only 7% grid fill. The body gradient provides temporal ordering of segments but cannot encode spatial reachability.

## Merged next experiments (prioritized)

1. **Add flood-fill reachability channel.** Compute reachable cell count from the head via BFS (ignoring segments about to expire based on body gradient). Encode as a 4th observation channel or scalar. This directly addresses topology blindness -- the primary failure mode. Both reports agree spatial awareness is missing; this is the most targeted fix.

2. **Switch distance shaping to potential-based form.** Replace the current delta-distance reward with gamma * phi(s') - phi(s) where phi = -manhattan_distance. Potential-based shaping is policy-invariant and eliminates the staircase bias while preserving the food-approach learning signal. If too disruptive, alternatively remove shaping entirely and rely on food reward + step penalty.

3. **Add explicit tail marker.** Either add a 4th binary channel for the tail cell, or increase the minimum body gradient value (e.g., floor at 0.1 instead of 1/n). This is low-cost and addresses Gemini's valid concern about tail vanishing under ReLU, even though it is not the primary failure mode. Can be combined with experiment 1.

## Recommendation

The primary bottleneck is spatial planning. The body gradient hypothesis was validated (zigzag eliminated, +80% performance), but the next capability gap requires topological information the current observations cannot provide. Prioritize adding a flood-fill reachability signal (experiment 1) as a new observation channel. Combine with experiment 3 (tail marker) as a low-risk addition. Address staircase bias (experiment 2) in a follow-up if the diagonal pattern persists after the observation augmentation.
