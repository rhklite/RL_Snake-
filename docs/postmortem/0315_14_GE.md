POSTMORTEM DIAGNOSIS
====================

## Run summary
- Run: runs/0315_14
- Duration: 6.0 hours (time limit reached)
- Best reward: 27.82 at update 4229
- Final reward: 23.78

## What worked
- **Survival and Growth**: The agent successfully learned to avoid walls and survive for extended periods, with average episode length increasing from ~100 to ~1000 steps. Average snake length increased from 1 to over 40.
- **Stable Training Dynamics**: Entropy decayed healthily from 1.38 to 0.50 without premature collapse. Policy and value losses remained stable throughout the run.
- **Spatial Awareness**: The agent demonstrates excellent spatial management, avoiding self-collisions even when taking up a large portion of the 32x32 grid.

## What failed
- **Suboptimal Pathfinding**: The agent learned a highly conservative "lawnmower" or "zig-zag" pattern. It systematically sweeps the board to guarantee eventual food capture while minimizing risk, rather than taking the shortest path to the food.
- **Inefficiency**: Because of this sweeping behavior, the agent takes a massive number of unnecessary steps, making reward accumulation very slow.
- **Late-game Body Collisions**: As the snake gets very long, death by body collision overtakes wall collisions (reaching ~57% of deaths), suggesting the rigid sweeping pattern eventually breaks down or makes a mistake at extreme lengths.

## Behavior vs expectation
- Expected: The agent should efficiently navigate directly to the food while avoiding obstacles and its own body.
- Observed: The agent uses a systematic, space-filling sweeping pattern (approximating a Hamiltonian cycle) to guarantee survival and eventual food capture, ignoring efficiency.
- Gap: The agent is overly conservative, prioritizing infinite survival over speed. This indicates the reward structure does not sufficiently penalize time spent or reward direct paths.

## Next experiments (max 3)
1. **Introduce a Step Penalty**: Add a small negative reward (e.g., -0.01) for each step taken to encourage the agent to find the shortest path to the food rather than safely sweeping the board.
2. **Increase Distance-Based Shaping**: Increase `dist_shaping_alpha` (currently 0.01) to provide stronger dense rewards for moving closer to the food, which will actively discourage moving away from the food during zig-zag sweeps.
3. **Scale Up Food Reward**: Make the reward for eating food significantly larger relative to any implicit survival rewards, shifting the policy's focus from "staying alive" to "eating quickly".

## Recommendation
Adjust rewards to penalize unnecessary steps and increase distance-based shaping to break the agent out of the conservative sweeping local optimum.
