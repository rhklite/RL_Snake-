POSTMORTEM DIAGNOSIS
====================

## Run summary

- Run: 0316_01_v2 (divine-fire-22)
- Config: 32x32 grid, hybrid arch (CNN + MLP), 4 layers, 128 hidden, 256 envs
- Duration: ~5.9 hours, 5715 updates, 187M timesteps (18.7% of budget)
- Best return: 49.94 at update 5319
- Best snake length: 83.1 at update 5306
- Final return (last 500 avg): 40.91 +/- 3.51
- Status: still training (PID 40182), but returns appear plateaued since ~update 4000
- Baseline comparison (0315_14): best return 27.82, best snake 46.1 in 6 hours

## Hypothesis

Body gradients along the snake body plus individual observation channels per element (head, body, foot) should help the CNN infer direction of travel, body length, and which blocks free up fastest. Combined with a deeper 4-layer network, this should solve the zigzag reward-hacking behavior seen in the previous run. Training runs indefinitely until plateau.

## What worked

- **Zigzag reward hacking eliminated.** The tight back-and-forth oscillation seen in the baseline run is gone. The snake moves purposefully toward food with smooth directional transitions.
- **Significant performance improvement.** Best return improved from 27.82 to 49.94 (+80%). Best snake length improved from 46 to 83 (+80%). The body gradient and per-element channels clearly provide the CNN with better spatial understanding.
- **Healthy training dynamics.** Entropy settled at ~0.40 (healthy exploration without collapse). Clip fraction stayed low at 0.01-0.02 (stable updates). Policy and value losses remained controlled. SPS degraded gracefully from 10.3k to 8.0k as episodes lengthened.
- **Death distribution shift.** Wall deaths dropped from 100% to 43%, body deaths rose to 56%. This is a positive signal: the snake is surviving long enough to face self-collision as the primary challenge rather than wandering into walls.

## What failed

- **Snake dies at only 7% grid fill.** Best episode reached length 72 on a 1024-cell grid. The snake gets trapped in narrow corridors between its own body segments. The body gradient tells the CNN which segments decay first but cannot encode topological connectivity or reachability.
- **Diagonal staircase movement pattern.** The tight zigzag is gone but a diagonal staircase pattern (alternating right-down or left-up steps) is pervasive. The distance shaping reward (alpha=0.01) actively incentivizes this: when food is diagonal, alternating steps each reduce Manhattan distance by 1, collecting the shaping bonus every step, whereas an L-shaped path would only collect the bonus on one leg.
- **Possible plateau at 18.7%.** Returns have been oscillating between 35-48 since around update 4000 with no clear upward trend. The policy has learned to eat food efficiently but has not learned body-topology management. Further training may not break through this barrier without observation or reward changes.

## Training dynamics detail

- **Reward trajectory:** Rapid improvement from -3.5 to +25 in first 20% of logged updates, then steady climb to 40-48 range. High variance (std 3.5 over last 500 updates) but no clear upward trend in the final third.
- **Entropy:** Dropped from 1.39 to ~0.40 by update 300 and held stable. Not collapsed (would be near 0) but notably lower than baseline final (0.50). The higher ent_coef of 0.03 (vs baseline's implied default) is helping maintain exploration.
- **Clip fraction:** Started at 0.04-0.07 during rapid learning, settled to 0.01-0.02. Updates are conservative and stable.
- **SPS:** Gradual decline from 11k to 8k, proportional to increasing episode length. No sudden drops or memory issues.
- **Episode length:** Grew from ~100 to ~1600 steps, tracking snake length closely (~23 steps per food eaten, near-optimal for 32x32 Manhattan distance).
- **Death breakdown over training:** Wall deaths dropped monotonically from 100% to 43%. Body deaths rose from 0% to 56%. Timeout deaths negligible (0.1%). The crossover happened around update 4000, coinciding with the apparent plateau.

## Behavior vs expectation

- **Expected:** Body gradients enable the CNN to reason about which blocks free up fastest, enabling efficient navigation and long survival without degenerate strategies.
- **Observed:** The snake navigates well, eats food efficiently (~23 steps/food), grows to length 60-83, then consistently dies from body collision in narrow corridors. Wall-hugging is common. No tight zigzag, but diagonal staircase patterns are frequent.
- **Gap 1 (topology blindness):** The body gradient provides local information about segment age but the CNN cannot reason about large-scale topology (enclosed regions, corridor exits, reachable cells). Even with 4 conv layers, the effective receptive field may not span the full grid. The snake walks into dead-end corridors it cannot detect as traps.
- **Gap 2 (staircase bias):** The distance shaping reward creates a subtle preference for staircase paths over L-paths. This is not as destructive as zigzag but wastes spatial layout by creating sawtooth body shapes that fragment free space.
- **Gap 3 (sparse death signal):** The -1.0 death penalty provides no gradient about how the snake trapped itself. The value function would need to learn that certain body topologies are dangerous many steps before the actual death, which is a hard credit assignment problem.

## Next experiments (max 3)

1. **Add a flood-fill reachability channel.** Compute the number of reachable cells from the head (via BFS/DFS ignoring body segments about to expire) and encode as a normalized scalar or as a binary reachability map channel. This directly addresses topology blindness. Expected effect: the policy can learn to avoid creating enclosed regions, extending survival well beyond length 80.

2. **Remove or reformulate distance shaping.** Either remove dist_shaping_alpha entirely (rely on food reward + step penalty) or switch to potential-based shaping (reward = gamma * phi(s') - phi(s) where phi = -manhattan_distance). Potential-based shaping is policy-invariant and would eliminate the staircase bias while still providing learning signal. Expected effect: straighter paths, less body fragmentation.

3. **Add an auxiliary reward for open space.** Give a small positive reward proportional to the flood-fill count from the head (e.g., 0.001 * reachable_cells / total_cells). This provides a continuous gradient signal for maintaining escape routes, complementing the sparse death penalty. Expected effect: the snake learns to keep large contiguous free space, delaying the body-collision failure mode.

## Recommendation

Augment the observation space with topological reachability information (flood-fill channel) and reformulate the distance shaping reward. The body gradient hypothesis was validated (zigzag eliminated, performance doubled) but the next bottleneck is spatial planning, which requires information the current observations cannot provide.
