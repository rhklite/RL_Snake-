## What worked

- Food-seeking behavior is strong. The agent reliably turns toward food and eats it at a rate of ~1 pellet per 22-25 steps.
- Entropy is healthy — ent_coef=0.03 prevents premature collapse while allowing meaningful policy sharpening.
- Clip fraction and learning rate are well-tuned — no sign of destructive updates or excessive conservatism.
- The hybrid architecture (CNN + food offset) processes the grid and directional cue effectively for basic navigation.
- Training is still improving at cutoff — more time would yield further gains.

## What failed

- **Zigzag/staircase locomotion persists**. The dominant movement pattern is a diagonal staircase (alternating horizontal-vertical steps). This wastes steps and creates fragmented body layouts. The -0.025 step penalty (2.5x the distance shaping signal) did not eliminate it. Shelved-ideas item #3 set a revisit condition at 15-20% progress — we are at 14.1% and the staircase is still the default strategy.
- **42.5% wall deaths at 14% progress**. The snake still frequently walks into walls, particularly from staircase trajectories heading toward corners where there is no room to turn.
- **Body-avoidance is weak**. 57.4% body deaths. The video shows self-collision even at length 6-11 in final episodes, where spatial pressure is zero — this is a pure policy failure, not a grid-capacity issue.
- **No space-efficient strategy**. No evidence of Hamiltonian-path-like sweeps, boustrophedon patterns, or tail-following. The snake creates ad-hoc coils that trap it around length 40-45 (4% grid occupancy).
- **CNN compression bottleneck**. The CNN applies one MaxPool (32 -> 16) then projects 8192 features to 128 dimensions — a 64:1 compression ratio. Fine-grained spatial relationships (e.g., "am I one cell from my body?") may be lost.

## Behavior vs expectation

- Expected (from shelved-ideas #3): zigzag should decrease under -0.025 step penalty by 15-20% progress.
- Observed: staircase movement is still the dominant locomotion pattern at 14.1% progress. The agent takes indirect routes, sometimes moving away from food before course-correcting.
- Gap: The step penalty is not sufficient to break the staircase habit. The policy converged to this pattern early (entropy collapsed to 0.50 by update 100) and subsequent learning refines food-seeking within this pattern rather than discovering straighter paths.
- Hypothesized cause: Distance shaping (+/-0.01 per step) rewards moving closer by Manhattan distance. Diagonal staircase movement achieves this — each "toward" step earns +0.01 while the perpendicular step is neutral (same distance). The step penalty (-0.025) should discourage unnecessary steps, but the food reward (+1.0) dominates the return, so the agent prioritizes food-reaching reliability over step-efficiency. The shaping signal is too weak to counteract the learned staircase.

## Next experiments (max 3)

1. **Extend training time to 12-18 hours**. The return curve is still rising. More updates may let the policy discover body-avoidance strategies that only become high-value at longer snake lengths. This is the lowest-risk intervention — change nothing except max_hours.

2. **Increase distance shaping alpha to 0.05 and reduce step penalty to -0.01**. The current ratio (step_penalty / shaping_alpha = 2.5) penalizes all movement but barely differentiates efficient from inefficient paths. Flipping the ratio (0.2) would make food-approach the dominant per-step signal, encouraging straight-line paths. Risk: could destabilize early training.

3. **Add a survival bonus that scales with snake length**. Currently the reward structure has no incentive to stay alive longer once food is consumed. A small bonus like +0.001 * snake_length per step would make longer snakes more "afraid" of dying, potentially encouraging more conservative body-avoidance at higher lengths without affecting early-game food-seeking. This directly addresses the body-collision problem.

## Recommendation

Extend training time first (experiment 1) to see if the current trajectory saturates. If it plateaus below avg_return 30 or body-death stays above 50%, run experiment 3 (survival bonus) to address the body-collision failure mode. Adjust the reward shaping ratio (experiment 2) only after determining whether the staircase pattern persists with more training time.
